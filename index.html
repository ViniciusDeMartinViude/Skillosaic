How it works

  1. 1
    Upload an image

    Drop or select any photo. It is read entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded to a server.

  2. 2
    Convert to LAB colour space

    Each pixel is converted from sRGB → XYZ → L*a*b*. LAB separates lightness (L) from colour information (a, b), making clustering perceptually uniform.

  3. 3
    Isolate black & white

    Pixels with L below the Black threshold or above the White threshold are pre-assigned to dedicated clusters before clustering begins.

  4. 4
    K-Means on a & b channels

    The remaining pixels are clustered using K-Means++ (K = 5) applied only to the a and b channels. Lightness is intentionally ignored so colour grouping is independent of brightness.

  5. 5
    Reconstruct the mosaic

    Each pixel keeps its original L value but its a and b are replaced by its cluster centroid. The result is converted back to sRGB and displayed.

  6. 6
    Palette, contours & smoothing

    Click palette swatches to isolate specific colour groups. Toggle Contours to see only cluster boundaries. Use the Smoothing slider to merge small regions via a mode filter.
